# Quantum Chemistry Visualizations (Mathematica)

A collection of interactive Wolfram Mathematica notebooks designed to visualize fundamental concepts in Elementary Quantum Chemistry. These animations and models provide a tangible way to understand abstract mathematical solutions to the Schrödinger equation.

## Overview
This repository contains a series of `.nb` files covering key topics in quantum mechanics. Each notebook is designed to be interactive, allowing users to manipulate parameters and observe real-time changes in wavefunctions and probability densities.

## Included Notebooks
* **Atomic Orbitals:** Visualization of hydrogen-like wavefunctions and radial/angular probability densities.
* **Particle in a Box:** Interactive models for 2D and 3D infinite potential wells.
* **Harmonic Oscillator:** Visual representations of quantum harmonic oscillator states and the Morse potential comparison.
* **Finite Well:** Solutions for tunneling and bounded states in finite potential barriers.
* **Ring & Sphere:** Rotational motion models and angular momentum visualizations.

## How to Use
1. **Software Required:** You will need [Wolfram Mathematica](https://www.wolfram.com/mathematica/) or the free [Wolfram Player](https://www.wolfram.com/player/) to view and interact with these notebooks.
2. **Download:** Clone this repository or download the specific `.nb` files you need.
3. **Interact:** Open the files and use the `Manipulate` sliders to change quantum numbers ($n, l, m$) or physical parameters.

## Educational Context
These tools were originally curated and developed to support coursework in **Elementary Quantum Chemistry**, helping students bridge the gap between algebraic derivations and physical intuition.
